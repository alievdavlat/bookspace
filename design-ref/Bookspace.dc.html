Between the lines

The library held its breath as evening light slipped through the tall windows. Nora ran a finger along the spines, each one a door to a life she had not lived...

...and somewhere among them was the story she had been waiting for. The clock above the desk paused, as clocks do in places where time is kept by pages rather than hands.

She pulled one free, and the room exhaled.

The Midnight Library
Matt Haig · Chapter 3

choice. The library was infinite, and somewhere within it was a version of her life where she had said yes. Nora pulled the green volume from the shelf and felt its weight settle in her hands.

She had spent so long mourning the lives she had not chosen that she had forgotten to live the one she had. The only way to learn is to live, the book seemed to whisper.

Outside, the snow had not yet stopped falling, and time, as always in the Midnight Library, refused to move.

42

When she opened it, the words rearranged themselves into a morning she half-remembered: salt air, a harbour, a name she had not spoken aloud in years.

It would be easy, she thought, to stay here forever — flipping between possibilities, never committing to a single page. But pages, like lives, are only meaningful when turned.

So she turned it.
